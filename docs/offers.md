# Offers

## Offer Strategy

GoPinion Outbound OS starts with two offers:

- Reviews / Reputation
- NAPS

For v1, Reviews / Reputation is the cleaner front-door offer. It maps more directly to public pain signals, is easier to explain in cold outreach, and is a stronger starting point for dealer qualification.

NAPS should be supported, but usually after the system can reliably identify fit, route the right message, and handle a second offer path without adding noise.

## Reviews / Reputation

### What Problem It Solves

This offer addresses weak or inconsistent reputation management, low review response discipline, and missed trust-building opportunities that affect perceived credibility and appointment readiness.

### Who It Fits Best

Best for dealers with:

- visible Google review volume
- middling ratings, especially around 3.2-4.0
- low or inconsistent review response
- obvious room to improve trust signals
- a growth-minded owner or GM who understands the business impact of reputation

### Signs That Indicate Fit

- rating below strong local competitors
- 15-150 reviews with uneven momentum
- negative or ignored reviews
- stale or inconsistent owner responses
- website and GBP both active enough to support improvement

### First Outreach Angle

Lead with visible external pain and missed opportunity:

- review response gap
- trust gap
- missed reputation lift
- credibility impact on shoppers

Keep the language concrete and operational, not abstract. The strongest opening angle is that there is a measurable reputation gap that can be improved without adding operational burden.

### CTA

Use a light appointment CTA:

- invite them to a short review of their current reputation situation
- offer to show where the visible gaps are
- position the meeting as a practical working session, not a high-pressure demo

### Offer Timing

This should be the first offer in v1 for most qualified accounts.

### Pricing Notes

Pricing is not defined yet in this repo. The product should treat Reviews / Reputation as the primary acquisition offer first, then layer pricing detail later once the v1 workflow is validated.

## NAPS

### What Problem It Solves

NAPS addresses post-sale follow-up and review aggregation needs for dealers that already understand the value of reputation systems and are ready for a more specific solution path.

### Who It Fits Best

Best for dealers with:

- enough operational maturity to understand post-sale follow-up value
- existing software usage
- some evidence they can adopt a structured workflow
- an owner or GM who is open to systems that improve customer follow-through and reputation outcomes

### Signs That Indicate Fit

- dealer already values reviews but execution is inconsistent
- signs of fragmented follow-up after sales
- enough customer volume to benefit from aggregation
- operator is practical and ROI-aware

### First Outreach Angle

Lead with operational improvement:

- easier aggregation
- more consistent post-sale follow-up
- clearer systemization around review and response flow

This angle should be tighter and slightly more informed than the Reviews front-door angle. It is better once some trust or relevance has already been established.

### CTA

Use a short appointment CTA focused on fit:

- offer a quick conversation on whether the current follow-up process is leaving review and reputation value on the table
- keep the ask simple and ROI-oriented

### Offer Timing

This is usually a later offer in v1, not the default first touch.

### Pricing Notes

- setup price: $699
- aggregates: $99/month

These numbers should be captured in the offer configuration later, but for now they belong in the planning layer so outbound logic can eventually reference them.
