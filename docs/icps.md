# ICPs

## Primary Market

GoPinion Outbound OS starts with U.S. independent used car dealerships.

This market is attractive for v1 because it offers:

- a clear, narrow starting segment
- visible public signals for qualification
- recurring reputation and follow-up problems
- a decision cycle that can often be influenced through direct outreach

## Dream Customer Summary

Best-fit dealers typically look like this:

- independent used car dealer
- 11-25 cars sold per month
- website present
- claimed Google Business Profile present
- Google rating around 3.2-4.0
- 15-150 reviews
- low or inconsistent review response
- growth-oriented, pain-aware, or solution-aware
- owner-operated or GM-led
- typically age 38-52
- already uses at least 2 SaaS tools

## Secondary Customer Summary

Secondary-fit dealers are similar, but usually slower-moving and more pragmatic:

- independent used car dealer
- website and Google Business Profile still present
- Google rating around 3.5-4.3
- 30-200 reviews
- less digitally active or less urgent
- likely age 40-58
- may need more trust-building and simpler offers

## Do Not Target

Avoid prioritizing:

- dealers with no website and no Google Business Profile
- dealers under 5 cars sold per month
- accounts already deeply locked into Birdeye or Podium
- dealers with 4.8+ stars and no visible pain
- corporate franchise groups
- retiring or highly tech-resistant operators

## Tier Breakdown

### Tier 1

Highest priority accounts.

Signals:

- clear independent dealer profile
- 11-25 cars per month
- website and claimed Google Business Profile present
- Google rating around 3.2-4.0
- 15-150 reviews
- low or inconsistent review response
- visible signs of growth intent or operational pain
- likely reachable owner or GM

Operating guidance:

- prioritize for first-pass outreach
- use the Reviews / Reputation offer first
- move quickly with personalized email and a short follow-up sequence

### Tier 2

Good accounts, but less urgent or slightly less aligned.

Signals:

- dealer profile is real and reachable
- website and GBP present
- rating roughly 3.5-4.3
- 30-200 reviews
- pain is present but less sharp
- operator appears practical rather than actively growth-driven

Operating guidance:

- keep in campaign rotation
- personalize less deeply than Tier 1
- lead with the most visible pain or missed opportunity

### Tier 3

Low priority or low-confidence accounts.

Signals:

- weak fit against dream profile
- limited visible pain
- unclear ownership or contactability
- signs of resistance, low urgency, or limited software readiness

Operating guidance:

- deprioritize or hold for later
- only engage if acquisition cost is very low
- do not let Tier 3 consume v1 workflow attention

## Decision-Maker Hypotheses

The most likely decision-makers for v1 are:

- owner
- operator-owner
- general manager
- dealership manager with direct responsibility for reputation or customer follow-up

Decision-maker inference should assume these are hypotheses, not facts. The system should store confidence and supporting signals such as title clues, company size, website language, public profiles, and review response behavior.

## Likely Pains

Common pains for the target segment:

- inconsistent review generation
- low or missing review response discipline
- underperforming Google Business Profile presence
- weak follow-up systems after sale
- limited time to run structured marketing operations
- scattered software stack with no unified outbound motion
- desire for more leads, trust, and appointment flow without adding major headcount

## Likely Objections

Common objections the product and outreach should expect:

- "We already have something for reviews."
- "We do not need another tool."
- "I am too busy for this right now."
- "Email outreach usually feels generic."
- "I do not know if this will work for our store."
- "We are small, so I do not want a complicated setup."

## Likely Proof Angles

The strongest proof angles for this ICP are:

- visible public review and response gaps
- simple before-and-after reputation improvement narrative
- operational clarity rather than marketing fluff
- low-friction setup
- realistic owner or GM workflow fit
- evidence that the system helps book appointments, not just send messages

## Channel Recommendations By Tier

### Tier 1

- email first
- light follow-up sequence
- optional manual research touch for high-confidence accounts

### Tier 2

- email first
- slightly broader messaging
- slower follow-up cadence

### Tier 3

- low-touch email only if acquisition cost is low
- otherwise suppress or hold

## Why Email-First Is The Right V1 Approach

Email-first is the right v1 motion because it is the cleanest channel for a modular outbound system:

- it is straightforward to model in sequences and campaigns
- it supports personalization without requiring heavyweight integrations
- it creates structured reply data for classification and learning
- it is operationally cheaper than multi-channel expansion
- it matches the need to test messaging, offers, and ICP quality before broadening channels
- it keeps the system focused on the core loop of targeting, outreach, response, booking, and learning

Phone, SMS, and richer multi-channel orchestration can come later, but email-first gives the best signal-to-complexity ratio for the current repo scope.
